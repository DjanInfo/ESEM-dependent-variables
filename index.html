An experiment on how dependent variables affect program comprehensibility

  

Author : Anonymous

Abstract :
\#ifdefs affect comprehensibility, especially when its use implies feature dependency. Feature dependencies differ from each other in terms of the types, number of dependent variables, the number of uses of each of these variables, variability, functions, data flow, control-flow, among others. The dependency between two features may include just one dependent variable or more than one. It is reasonable to suspect that a high number of dependent variables and their uses make the analysis of variability scenarios more complex. In this sense, it is also important to investigate if the comprehensibility of configurable systems varies according to the number of dependent variables present in the source code. Thus, we analyzed how different numbers of dependent variables affected the comprehensibility of configurable systems. We executed a controlled experiment with 12 participants who analyzed programs trying to specify the output. We quantified comprehensibility using metrics based on time and tentative to answer tasks correctly, participantsâ€™ visual effort and participants' heart rate. Our results indicate that the higher the number of dependent variables the more difficult it was to understand programs with feature dependency.

 
 
Procedure:

1 - we cleaned all material and equipment with alcohol gel and asked the participant to fill out a consent form;

3 - calibrate eye-tracking and smartwatch devices;
4 - we asked her or him to watch a two minute full-screen video of a fish swimming in an aquarium;
5 - perform the task in order based in Latin square group;
